# Claims

## Supported Claims

- The repository implements a real local MuJoCo data-engine benchmark with physics-derived failure labels and acquisition rounds.
- Failure-stratified acquisition increases some rare-failure recall measurements.
- The combined-tail benchmark exposes a real failure mode for the proposed idea: better rare-label coverage does not necessarily produce better robust policy selection.
- The package is reproducible from local code, CSV artifacts, figures, and a rebuilt PDF.

## Unsupported Claims

- Do not claim ICLR-main readiness.
- Do not claim state-of-the-art robot data-engine performance.
- Do not claim real-robot validation.
- Do not claim the proposed failure-stratified engine beats active failure prediction, uncertainty sampling, or random sampling on the decisive robust-success metric.

## Terminal Claim

Paper 74 is a useful negative result: mechanism-aware failure coverage is not sufficient to improve tail-robust robot data selection in this benchmark.
